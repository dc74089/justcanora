Learn how to make your own website!

Join us for Websites & The Internet to fulfill your Innovation Institute requirement!

This Course Teaches:

  • HTML Programming
  • Website Design
  • How the Internet Works (basic computer networking)
  • General Technology/MacBook Skills

Course Highlights:

  • Publish Real Websites on the Internet!
  • Build a Personal Homepage, or Resume/Portfolio
  • Design & Build a Choose Your Own Adventure Game
  • Create a Website for a Product
  • Build a Virtual Pet

From the Curriculum Guide:

Step into the fascinating world of web design and development! In this hands-on project-based course, students will grasp the basics of designing and programming websites using HTML while exploring the inner workings of the internet. We will utilize the design process to conceptualize, design, program, deploy, and deliver multiple websites throughout the semester. In addition, students will develop and strengthen general computer skills which will help them become better users of technology in all aspects of their lives.

Take the next step in Computer Science!

Prerequisite: Successful completion of MS Computer Science 1.

Want to move to the next level of Computer Science and learn a professional, text-based programming language? We'd love to have you join us for Programming in Python!

This Course Teaches:

Programming in Python, including:
  • Basic Syntax
  • Variables
  • Lists
  • Loops
  • Dictionaries
  • Text-Based User Interfaces

Course Highlights:

  • Learn an industry-standard multipurpose programming language
  • Compete in programming competitions against your classmates
  • Use the same tools as professional programmers
  • Build a solid foundation for Upper School CS courses

From the Curriculum Guide:

Programming in Python will continue to develop on the fundamentals developed in CS I. The primary focus will be on evolving students understanding of key computer programming concepts (variables, data structures, control structures, Boolean logic, and syntax). Students will use the Python programming language to develop effective and efficient software solutions to a variety of relevant and engaging programming challenges. Although the class will be taught in Python the fundamentals learned are applicable to all programming languages.

Build your Public Speaking skills!

In every class and every career, solid Public Speaking skills will always be an asset. Join us as we strengthen our communication skills in an inclusive, supportive environment.

This Course Teaches:

  • General Communication Skills
  • Speaking in different contexts:
    • Speaking to Inform
    • Speaking to Persuade
    • Speaking to Entertain

Course Highlights:

  • Build skills that will serve you for the rest of your life
  • Learn to communicate effectively and deliver your message clearly
  • Practice constructing and delivering effective arguments
  • Work collaboratively and constructively with your peers

From the Curriculum Guide

Public Speaking is a semester-long class dedicated to the development of public speaking and presentation skills. The students will learn about the processes of communication including both verbal and non-verbal communication. Informative, persuasive, and argumentative speeches, among others, will give students experience with various forms of speaking and will hone advanced speaking and delivery skills. Students will also learn strategies for practical, real-world and 21st century speaking scenarios, such as job interviews, podcasts, and conversations with adults. The objective of this course is to develop speaking and delivery skills that students will use in the Upper School, college, and beyond.
